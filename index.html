Who I Am
About Me

I'm Karthik Natarajan, a third-year Bioinformatics student at the University of Saskatchewan. I've spent my time here not just studying, but paying close attention to the students around me.

I've watched too many of my peers reach a breaking point. When a university doesn't offer enough support or things to do, people find other ways to cope. I've seen fellow students turn to alcohol and drugs not out of fun, but out of boredom, loneliness, and a lack of real resources.

That's not okay. And it doesn't have to be this way. I'm running because I believe every student deserves a campus that actually invests in their wellbeing — not just their grades.

What I Stand For
My Platform
01
Mental Health
No student should have to wait weeks for support or feel like they have nowhere to turn.
  • Peer support networks in every faculty
  • Mental health days — recognised, not penalised
  • Destigmatising conversations on campus
  • Peer-to-peer support program — a student in every building every Tuesday & Thursday who you can walk up to and talk to
02
Campus Life
A vibrant campus gives students a reason to stay, connect, and belong.
  • More free & affordable social events
  • Better spaces for students to gather & unwind
  • Support for student clubs and initiatives
  • Programming that reflects our diverse community
03
Student Voice
Your concerns shouldn't disappear after an election. I'll make sure they don't.
  • Regular open town halls & feedback sessions
  • Transparent updates on every commitment made
  • A direct line to administration for student issues
  • Representation for international & commuter students
No Vague Promises
3 Things I'm Committing To
01
Peer Support Pop-Ups

In the library during midterms and finals. No appointments, no waitlists — just someone there when you actually need it.

02
A Free Weekly Social

On campus every week. Doesn't revolve around drinking, doesn't require joining a club — genuinely for every student, especially the ones who feel like USask is just a place they pass through.

03
"You Said, We Did" Board

Every semester I publish exactly what students told me and exactly what I did about it. No more feedback disappearing into nothing.
